Biography

William Harrison Smith was born in the heart of London during the final decade of the Victorian era. The son of a local blacksmith, he grew up with a keen interest in mechanical engineering, which eventually led him to enlist as a technician during the First World War.

After the war, William sought new opportunities across the Atlantic. He emigrated to New York in 1922, arriving via Ellis Island with only a small trunk and his drafting tools. It was here that he met Sarah Jenkins, whom he married in 1925 at St. Patrick's Cathedral.

William spent three decades working for the New York Port Authority, contributing to the expansion of the city's infrastructure. He was known among his peers for his meticulous attention to detail and his collection of hand-drawn mechanical schematics, many of which are preserved in the family archives today.
